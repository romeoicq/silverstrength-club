Simple Resistance Band Routine for Seniors

A full-body, joint-friendly workout that builds strength safely — can be done seated or standing.

By SilverStrength Club

Published: May 14, 2026

Resistance bands are one of the safest ways to build strength after 65. This 5-exercise routine works your whole body without stressing your joints.

  1. Banded Chair Row — Loop band around feet, pull toward chest. 10-12 reps. Targets upper back and posture.
  2. Seated Leg Press — Loop band around soles, push legs out. 10-12 reps. Strengthens legs.
  3. Banded Bicep Curls — Stand on band, curl hands to shoulders. 10-12 reps.
  4. Standing Hip Abduction — Band around ankles, lift leg sideways. 8-10 reps per side.
  5. Banded Overhead Press — Stand on band, press overhead. 8-10 reps.
Start Light: Use a light resistance band (yellow/red). 1-2 sets, 10 reps. Rest 60s between exercises. Do 2-3x per week.
