7 Nutrition Tips for Healthy Aging

Simple, practical eating advice for energy, brain health, and longevity — starting today.

Good nutrition is one of your most powerful tools for healthy aging. These 7 practical tips will help you eat well for energy, brain health, and longevity.

  • Eat enough protein — 20-30g per meal. Eggs, yogurt, fish, chicken, lentils, tofu. Preserves muscle mass.
  • Prioritize calcium & vitamin D — 1200mg calcium daily. Dairy, greens, fortified foods. Vitamin D from sunlight or supplements.
  • Include omega-3s — Salmon, walnuts, flaxseeds. Supports brain health and reduces inflammation.
  • Get plenty of fiber — Oats, beans, berries, vegetables. Supports digestion, blood sugar, and heart health.
  • Stay hydrated — 6-8 glasses of water daily. Thirst decreases with age. Add lemon or fruit for flavor.
  • Consider B12 — Many seniors have trouble absorbing B12 from food. Fortified cereals or supplements can help.
  • Eat with others — Social eating improves appetite and enjoyment. Share meals with family or community.
Weekly Goal: Add one colorful vegetable to every meal. Include protein at breakfast. Drink a glass of water with each meal.
